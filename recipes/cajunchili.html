Cajun Chili

This is a modification to a boilmakers tailgate chili recipe that I found online with a cajun twist. Crab boil gives it a distinct smokey flavor. The italian sausage was replaced with cajun sausage and tasso. Otherwise the ingredient list stays the same. I have not yet me someone who does not like this chili and would highly recommend it for your next get-together/function.

  • 2 pounds of ground beef chuck
  • 1 pound of cajun sausage
  • 1 pound of tasso
  • 3(15oz) cans chili beans, drained
  • 1(15oz) can chili beans in spicy sauce
  • 2(28oz) cans diced tomatoes with juice
  • 1(6oz) can tomato paste
  • 1 large yellow onion chooped
  • 1 green bell pepper seeded and chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper seeded and chopped
  • 2 green chili peppers seeded and chopped
  • 4 cubes of beef bouillon (or better than bouillon)
  • 1/2 cup of beer
  • 1/4 cup of chili powder
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp hot pepper sauce
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp cayenne powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 3 capfulls of crab boil
  1. Put all meat in bot and begin to brown.
  2. Once grease has evaporated, add in vegetables and seasonings.
  3. Let pot sit on low heat for 2-3 hours and then wait 30 minutes before serving.
